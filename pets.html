Adoptions

When CR paws gets cats and kittens that are deemed sociable and ready for adoption at intake
they are generally transferred over to the BC SPCA Campbell River Branch. From there they find suitable homes
for adoption. If you are looking for kittens please visit their website or contact them directly at
250-287-7766 or campbellriver@spca.bc.ca

However, often kittens and cats come into our care and require additional socialization before
they are ready for adoption. In that case we place them into one of our loving foster homes to
work on socialization. Once they are deemed adoptable, we puut them up for adoption and they
will be shown on our facebook page. If you are interested in adopting one of the cats/kittens listed please send us
a call through the Contact Us page of this website or via our page on facebook

Adoption Fees (Present Date)

Included in the adoption is a spray/neuter, their first vaccine, deworming and flea treatments, and a tattoo inside their ear

  • Kittens up to 8 months: $250
  • Kittens 9 months to 3 years: $200
  • Adult cats over 3 years: $125
  • Senior cats over 9 years/Special needs cats: $75

Lost and Found

Lost and Found Animals of Campbell River (LFACR) is a cooperative group with facebook communities
that was established in 2012. In 2016, LFACR joined another group of volunteers to create CR P.A.W.S.
Together we are working to help animals in our community.

The purpose of the facebook group is to give pet guardians an online location to post ads for lost
or escaped and found animals. The group is comprised of 4000 local, caring, pet advocates who will
then share the post, assist with location, and in most cases the return of beloved pets.

LFACR offers online visibility, equipment for retrieval of pets, and collaboration with local CR P.A.W.S.,
SPCA, and Animal Control to ensure the highest probability of lost and found returns. We also work
closely with other lost and found groups on Vancouver Island.

LFACR upholds the mission statement of CR P.A.W.S. to promote the well being and safety of animals
in Campbell Riber area through education. LFACR also does this through the recovery of lost pets, and maintenance of feral
cat colonies in the community. This includes TNR and liaison with other rescue organizations.

If you have lost and found an animal in the Campbell River area, join our facebook page and post
a photograph with as much detail and description as possible. Things to include are...

  • Photo
  • Name of animal
  • Breed, color, size
  • location of lost/found animal
  • Tattoo or any other form of identification
  • Contact info (name + phone number)
  • Any behaviour characteristics (friendly, timid, doesn't like other dogs, etc)
  • Remember to check your post regularly for updates and to update when the animal has been reunited with it's owner
